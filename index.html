Your Bi-Weekly Upgrade

Mastering the "Not Yet" Mindset

Move from seeing AI as a simple tool to using it as a powerful collaborator for strategy, creation, and problem-solving.

The Core Idea: The Frontier is Always Moving

"AI can't do this." "AI can't do this... yet."

Why This Mindset Matters

Our expectations for technology are linear, but AI progress is exponential. The gap between those two lines is where the advantage is found.

Your job is to be the first to notice when those limits disappear. This guide provides four missions. Choose **one** this week and spend 30 minutes on it.

Mission: The Strategist

The goal is to move from using AI as a search engine to using it as a free, world-class consultant that is available 24/7. Your job is to brief it like an expert, not just ask it simple questions.

Technique: Give it a Persona

Always start your prompt with "Act as a...". This provides crucial context and dramatically improves the quality of the response.

Example for SalesFam

// Try this in Google Gemini

"Act as a VP of Sales for a fast-growing SaaS startup. Our product, **SalesFam**, is a commission tracking platform. I've uploaded our last quarter's sales data. Analyze the data and identify the top 3 biggest risks to us hitting our goals next quarter. Provide a brief, actionable mitigation plan for each risk."

Example for SuperWill

// Try this in Anthropic's Claude

"Act as a digital marketing expert specializing in sensitive legal services. Our venture, **SuperWill**, is an automated legal will attorney. The target audience is young families who have never thought about a will. Create a content strategy outline for a blog designed to educate this audience without using fear-based tactics. Include 5 potential blog post titles."

Pro-Tip: Ask for Specific Formats

End your prompt by telling the AI how to structure the output. This saves you time on reformatting. Try adding phrases like "Provide the output as a markdown table," or "Summarize the findings in three clear bullet points."

Pro-Tip: Create a Dedicated "Project Brain"

Don't start a new chat for every small question about the same project. Keep all your brainstorming, planning, and questions for a single venture (like **hyperbuds**) in one continuous Gemini chat. Over time, the AI will build a deep contextual understanding of your project, making its future answers smarter, faster, and more relevant. Think of it as training your own specialist consultant.

Mission: The Prototyper

The goal is to move from "writing code" to "directing the creation of code." This crushes prototyping time from hours to minutes, allowing you to validate ideas instantly.

Technique: Live Previews with Artifacts

In Claude, when you ask for a UI component, it doesn't just give you code. It creates a live, interactive preview window called an "Artifact" that you can see and test immediately.

Example for xKPI

// Try this in Anthropic's Claude

"Create a simple, modern pricing dashboard for a B2B SaaS product called **xKPI**. It should have three tiers: Basic, Pro, and Enterprise. Use HTML and Tailwind CSS. Each tier should be a card showing the price, a key feature list, and a sign-up button. Make the 'Pro' tier the most visually prominent with a different color and a 'Most Popular' badge."

Example for Cyberly

// Try this in Anthropic's Claude

"Build a UI for a live video streaming app called **Cyberly**. It should have a main video window, a vertical chat box on the right, and a 'Send Tip' button below the video. The layout should be clean and modern. Use HTML and Tailwind CSS."

Pro-Tip: Iterative Refinement

The first result is just a draft. Treat the AI like a junior developer. Give it conversational feedback: "That's a good start, but make the buttons purple," or "Change the color scheme to a dark mode." Watch the Artifact update in real-time.

Pro-Tip: Build in Components

Don't ask for an entire complex webpage at once. Ask for individual components first (a navbar, a hero section, a feature grid). Once you're happy with each piece, ask the AI to combine them into a single, cohesive page.

Mission: The Scientist

The goal is to actively maintain a "Failure List" of tasks AI can't do, and re-test them with every new model release. This makes you the first to know when the impossible becomes possible.

Technique: Leverage Large Context Windows

Modern models can read and reason over hundreds of pages of text at once. Use this to your advantage by uploading large, complex documents and asking for high-level analysis.

Example for SuperWill

// Try this in Google AI Studio

"I've uploaded a dense 200-page legal document regarding probate law. Act as a senior legal counsel for **SuperWill**. Read the entire document and identify the top five most unusual or potentially exploitable clauses for our company. Explain each one simply."

Example for BioAdmin

// Try this in Google AI Studio

"I have uploaded a CSV file containing anonymized patient symptom data. Act as a medical data analyst for **BioAdmin**. Analyze this data and identify any potential correlations or patterns between symptoms that might indicate a common underlying issue. Present your findings as a series of clear, testable hypotheses."

Pro-Tip: The "What If" Prompt

Push the AI's creative and strategic limits by asking speculative questions. This is great for exploring new venture ideas. "What if we combined the business model of **Unhire** with the haptic technology of **Proxina**? What would be three potential product ideas for that merged company?"

Pro-Tip: Specify the Output Format for Analysis

When asking for data analysis, tell the AI exactly how to format the output. This makes the information immediately usable. "Analyze the uploaded user feedback for **Cheaper** and return a JSON object with three keys: 'positive_themes', 'negative_themes', and 'feature_requests'."

Mission: The Artist

The goal is to move from being a "requester" of creative work to a "director" of it. This empowers you to visualize your ideas instantly, allowing you to provide a much better brief to a professional designer later.

Technique: Descriptive Prompting

Don't just name a subject. Describe the style, mood, lighting, and composition. The more descriptive you are, the closer you'll get to your vision.

Example for Kwiks

// Try this in Leonardo.Ai

"A modern, minimalist logo for a social video sharing app called **Kwiks**. The design should feature a stylized 'K' that subtly incorporates a play button icon. The color scheme should be vibrant purple and white. Clean, vector style, on a black background."

Example for Elaview

// Try this in Microsoft Designer

"A photorealistic image for a billboard ad for our venture, **Elaview**. A smiling small business owner is standing in front of her coffee shop. She is pointing up at a digital billboard above her store that shows *her own advertisement*. The style should be bright, optimistic, and cinematic, with warm, golden hour lighting."

Pro-Tip: Iterate with Negative Prompts

Most image generators allow "negative prompts" to tell the AI what to avoid. If your images have weird hands or too much text, add a negative prompt like `--no deformed hands, text, words` to clean up the result.

Pro-Tip: Use AI for Mood Boarding

Before you brief a designer, generate 5-10 different styles for the same concept. Try adding keywords like "cinematic," "flat vector," "cyberpunk," "90s retro," or "claymation" to your prompt. This helps you communicate your visual direction much more effectively.
